## Fueling Context

- Historically struggles to fuel during exercise — sympathetic dominance at high HR closes the gut
- Core insight from June 6: when HR stays in Z2, food is appealing and gut works. Race fueling strategy depends entirely on HR management.
- Target on long efforts: 60–90g carbs/hour, starting within first 30 minutes
- Confirmed working: Hammer gels, aid station food, SweetTarts in a pinch
- Pre-diabetic approach: food composition over quantity; protein + fiber before carbs; whole carbs over refined; walk after eating
- Post-effort recovery window is a known gap — appetite suppressed after hard efforts, blood sugar chaos follows.
- **June 13 fueling:** a couple gels, Cheez-Its, a few mini SweetTarts, shared Coke with Nick, a few Enduralytes. Ad hoc but functional. Energy felt ok through the back half. Skipped a gel in the last 1.5 miles out of stubbornness — acknowledged mistake, don't repeat on race day.
- **Scratch High Carb soft flask:** identified as next fueling evolution. Pre-mixed flask (one serving ≈ 200 cal / 500ml) for sustained liquid carbs, especially on NCT where stopping isn't realistic. Supplement real food, don't replace it.
- **Aid station powder solution:** pre-portioned single-serving tubes (GoTubb or small zip-lock) in front vest pocket. Dump + water from station jug = ~20 sec. Practice this in training.

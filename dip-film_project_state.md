## PROJECT OVERVIEW

**Working Title:** Dips with the Girl Gang (or Daily Dips - TBD)

**Format:** Documentary short  
**Runtime:** ~8:30 (current assembly)  
**Aspect Ratio:** Mixed (vertical iPhone footage + horizontal GoPro + stills)

**Logline:**  
Two acquaintances meet at a Lake Superior beach for morning dips in September. As the water freezes and the stakes rise, their ritual becomes a lifeline—and attracts a third. A film about showing up, unexpected friendship, and finding light in the coldest season.

**Core Themes:**
- Mental health recovery through ritual
- Unexpected friendship as lifeline
- Grief sitting next to joy
- Physical commitment as emotional transformation
- The power of showing up

## SCRIPT / NARRATIVE STRUCTURE

### ACT 1 - SETUP (Sept)
- Cold open: extreme winter → "this is the thing that brought me back"
- Origin story: met Dawn at bike clinic 2021, acquaintances not close
- September begins: Dawn's boys, school drop-off ritual, beautiful weather
- Marty's state: injury, depression, stuck, "I didn't know how to be that person anymore"
- The invite: reacted to story, "join us tomorrow," kept coming

### ACT 2 - ESCALATION (Fall → Winter)
- Early joy: "just twenty minutes" - ritual established
- Dawn's grief: Tara's loss, lake as connection point
- Season change: "we said day by day" but kept going
- Winter commitment: neoprene, ice shelves, negative temps, wind squalls
- "What surprised me most... was how normal it all felt"
- **COMEDY BEAT** (visual relief)

### ACT 2.5 - EXPANSION (February)
- Kerasa joins: moved last summer, homesick, shows up anyway
- Three becomes a crew: "We call ourselves a girl gang"

### ACT 3 - TRANSFORMATION
- Daily ritual: talk through life / breathe and smile / "we live in a postcard"
- Internal shift: "I got lighter. I got braver. I started feeling grateful again."
- Core truth: "someone meets me here every day"
- **TURNING POINT:** No open water, went home dry
- **REVERSAL:** Hammers and rakes - "We said we'd never break ice... turns out there are no lines anymore"

### RESOLUTION
- The point isn't the water
- The point is showing up, friendship, grief + joy, walking into the day
- "I love us" / "Me too"

## CURRENT CREATIVE CHALLENGES

### ~~VOICE / TONE ISSUE~~ (RESOLVED - 2026-04-05)
**Problem:** Script was poetic but didn't sound like Marty. Rewriting in natural voice became too narrative.

**Solution:** Stopped trying to poeticize the injury section. Used Marty's actual words from conversation, just tightened. Key insight: specificity (plantar fasciitis, one foot then the other, shame and guilt) is more powerful than abstract poetic language. "That's what kept me sane" instead of clinical "fresh air and exercise kept depression at bay."

**Result:** Final version sounds authentic, hits harder emotionally, includes previously missing details (shame/guilt) that deepen the story.

**Current state:** Can't see how to cut it shorter without losing essential beats

**Trade-off:**
- Longer runtime = harder festival placement, viewer attention risk
- BUT story needs room to breathe—this isn't a montage, it's a journey
- 8:30 is defensible if every minute earns its place

**Next step:** Once assembly is complete, ruthlessly audit—does every section advance emotion or story? Where does it drag?

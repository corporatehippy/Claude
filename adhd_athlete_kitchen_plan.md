## 1. Client Profile

### Who You Are

- Adult with ADHD — challenges include decision fatigue, overthinking, and defaulting to easy/low-effort options (frozen pizza) when executive function is depleted
- Endurance athlete — training load is significant; fueling is a real consideration, not just convenience
- Works from home — this is a meaningful advantage; dutch oven and low-and-slow cooking are genuinely accessible on most days
- Cooking attitude: enjoys cooking but doesn't want to measure fussy ingredients; a 'measure with your heart' cook
- Self-described 'ingredient household' not a 'recipe household' — thinks in components, not dishes
- Cooks for two: herself and Nick. Nick is a more conservative eater, especially around salads (likes traditional greek, antipasto, would eat iceberg with thousand island happily). Salad planning needs to account for this — shared base, diverge on toppings, or build around common ground.

### Kitchen Strengths (Already Working)

- Instinctively uses formulas over recipes — the pasta bake (frozen meatballs + jarred sauce + dry pasta + oven) is a perfect example of this in action
- Loves and owns a dutch oven — already uses it for barbacoa, carnitas, Italian beef, salsa chicken
- Has a shopping rhythm: aims for every 2–3 weeks, prefers Saturday or Sunday evenings; currently stretches to 4 weeks and leans on takeout at the end of that cycle
- Comfortable with hands-off cooking — long oven/dutch oven times are not a problem, especially working from home
- Open to ingredient-level prep (not full meal prep) to reduce mid-week friction
- Good instinct for immediate ingredient prep: pulls rotisserie chicken off the carcass as soon as she gets home — this is exactly right and should be the model for all 'landing' tasks when groceries arrive
- Uses a vacuum sealer for freezer storage — proteins stored this way are reliable even long-term (confirmed: last summer's BBQ pulled pork still perfect)
- Owns a 22" Weber kettle grill — accessible once snow clears from deck (spring/summer cooking option)

### Current Friction Points

- Overthinking what to cook leads to decision fatigue → defaults to frozen pizza or takeout
- Versatile proteins (ground beef, chicken breasts) go in the cart without a committed purpose → open-ended at cook time → decision paralysis when executive function is gone
- Shopping trips stretching to 4 weeks → pantry gaps → more takeout at the end of cycles
- Dinner often happens around 9:30–10pm after work + workout + evening projects/gaming — meals need to be finishable, not startable, at that hour
- Lunches can fall apart during busy work weeks → entire weeks of ordering out

## 2. Frameworks We've Built

### The Formula Approach (Not Recipes)

Client thinks in formulas instinctively. Key insight: each formula is a template where the flavor profile can rotate without changing the process. This keeps weeknight cooking low-decision.

**Core Cooking Formulas**

| Formula | Structure | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Dutch Oven Dump | Fat + aromatics + protein + liquid + time | Barbacoa, carnitas, Italian beef, salsa chicken |
| Pasta Bake | Starchy base + protein + sauce + oven | Frozen meatballs + jarred sauce + dry pasta |
| Sheet Pan | Protein + veg + olive oil + seasoning → 400°F → 25 min | Chicken thighs + frozen veg + whatever seasoning |
| Grain Bowl | Cooked grain + protein + acid + fat + greens (optional) | Rice + leftover dutch oven meat + lemon + avocado |
| Quick Skillet | Fat + aromatics + protein + sauce or liquid → serve over grain | Sausage + onion + jarred tomatoes over pasta |
| Dump and Bake | Everything in the vessel + oven + time | Meatball pasta bake, baked gnocchi, white bean sausage bake |

### The Protein Defaults Framework

Key insight from session: the problem isn't planning — it's that versatile proteins go in the cart with no committed purpose, which means they're still an open question at 9:30pm when executive function is gone. The fix is a **two-second mental commit at the meat case**, not a full meal plan. Just assign a default direction before it hits the cart.

**At the meat case:**
- Chuck roast → barbacoa or Italian beef (decide when you cook it) — already doing this
- Chicken breasts → Foreman grill → fridge. Always. It's an ingredient, not a meal plan.
- Ground beef → default is Korean bowl, beef and bean skillet, or dirty rice — pick one before it goes in the cart. NOT spaghetti (she doesn't like it with ground beef; Italian sausage only for pasta).
- Ground pork → default is larb bowl or ginger pork rice bowl
- Italian sausage → pasta, white bean skillet, or sheet pan with peppers and onions

The protein in the cart = the default in your head. You're not committing to a night or a time — just a direction.

### The 'Ingredient Household' Stocking System

Goal: always have at least one item in each category so assembly is possible without a plan.

| Category | Always Have Something Like... |
|---|---|
| Cooked or easy protein | Rotisserie chicken (pull off bones immediately), canned fish, hard-boiled eggs, leftover dutch oven meat, deli meat, pre-cooked sausage, frozen shrimp |
| Cooked or quick grain | Leftover rice, farro, barley, pasta, tortillas, bread |
| Zero-prep vegetable | Frozen veg, pre-washed greens, cherry tomatoes, bagged slaw, canned beans |
| Sauce / flavor bomb | Jarred salsa, pasta sauce, miso, soy sauce, pesto, hot sauce, chipotles in adobo, Thai red curry paste, fish sauce |
| Fat | Olive oil, butter, avocado, tahini |
| Salad pantry | Kalamata olives, pepperoncini, roasted red peppers (jarred), dried cranberries, walnuts/slivered almonds, feta (in brine), pita chips, balsamic vinaigrette, Italian dressing |

*The salad pantry items are buy-once-keep-forever staples. With these on hand and pre-cooked chicken in the fridge, any salad takes 5 minutes.*

Protein chosen → short list of supporting ingredients auto-follows. Shopping decisions shrink to: which proteins this trip, and do I have what goes with them?

### Protein Planning for a 2–3 Week Cycle

Rather than planning specific meals, plan protein categories per trip.

- 1–2 dutch oven / roast proteins (chuck roast or pork shoulder — versatile, freeze well, stretch far)
- 1 quick weeknight protein (chicken breasts always → Foreman → fridge; ground meat or sausage with a default assigned)
- Pantry/freezer proteins that are always stocked (canned fish, eggs, frozen shrimp, pre-cooked meatballs)

*The pantry proteins are the safety net for week 3–4 when fresh proteins are gone.*

**Note on protein volume:** Don't overbuy raw proteins in a single trip unless you freeze intentionally on arrival. Too many open-ended proteins = too many open questions at cook time.

### Ingredient Prep (Not Meal Prep)

Highest-ROI things to do when you're already in the kitchen — not as a separate task:

- Pull rotisserie chicken off the carcass immediately when you get home — non-negotiable, already doing this
- Big batch of grains (rice, farro, barley) — 30-40 min hands-off, feeds the week
- Hard-boiled eggs — do while water is boiling for something else
- Foreman grill 2-3 chicken breasts on shopping day → slice → fridge → lunches for 4-5 days
- Roasted garlic or caramelized onions — batch when oven is already on, keeps all week
- Washed/dried greens — do this when unpacking groceries so friction disappears mid-week

## 3. The Dutch Oven as Weekly Anchor

Client's preferred approach: one dutch oven cook per week (often a Sunday or Monday when already home). 10 min active time, then hands-off. Whatever comes out becomes a rotating ingredient, not a single meal.

Example rotation from one cook:

- Night 1 — tacos or burritos from the main protein
- Night 2 — grain bowl with leftover protein
- Night 3 — eggs poached in leftover braising liquid or sauce
- Day-of — leftovers over greens for a quick lunch

*Key principle: the cooking is handled; the creativity is spontaneous. This works well for ADHD brains that resist rigid plans but do fine with loose 'what do I feel like tonight?' decisions.*

## 4. Training Day Fueling Strategy

On high-output training days: goal is assembly, not cooking. Decision fatigue is highest on these days; friction must be lowest.

- Use the stocked-category system to assemble without deciding — protein + grain + vegetable + sauce
- Post-training meals should be carbohydrate-forward — pre-cooked grain makes this automatic
- Aim for a real meal in under 5 minutes using what's already prepped

## 5. Meal Ideas Bank

Organized by effort tier. Living list — built out based on what she actually makes and likes.

### Tier 1 — Assembly / No Real Cooking

- Grain bowl: pre-cooked grain + protein + sauce + greens
- Tacos / burritos: leftover dutch oven meat + tortillas + whatever's around
- Snack plate dinner: deli meat, cheese, olives, crackers, fruit, hummus
- Canned fish over greens or with crackers and hot sauce
- Leftover anything + fried egg on top
- Any salad from the salad bank (see below) — if chicken is pre-cooked, 5 min max
- Kevin's Paleo sous vide chicken pouches over rice — confirmed winner, zero-friction, freezer staple

### Tier 2 — Quick Active Cook (20–30 min)

**Skillet meals — ground beef:**
- Korean beef bowl — brown, add soy/sesame oil/garlic/ginger/gochujang/honey, over rice, fried egg on top. 15 min.
- Beef and bean skillet — brown beef, dump in canned beans + canned tomatoes or salsa + cumin/chili powder. Eat with tortillas, over rice, or with chips. Crack an egg in at the end if you want.
- Dirty rice — brown beef with onion, lots of black pepper and cajun seasoning, stir into leftover rice in the same pan.

**Skillet meals — ground pork or turkey:**
- Larb bowl — cook loose, add fish sauce/lime/chili flake/pinch of sugar, over rice with cucumber. Fast and different.
- Ginger pork rice bowl — ground pork + garlic + ginger + soy + mirin, over rice. 15 min.
- Pork and kimchi skillet — brown pork, add kimchi + sesame oil, over rice. Zero thinking required if you keep kimchi around.

**Skillet meals — Italian sausage:**
- White bean and sausage skillet — slice or crumble sausage, brown, add white beans + splash of broth or white wine + greens if you have them. Crusty bread.
- Sausage, peppers, onions over polenta or grits
- Pasta with Italian sausage — the only ground-meat pasta she actually likes

**Skillet meals — chicken:**
- Coconut curry — cube chicken, brown, add curry paste + can of coconut milk, simmer 15 min. Over rice or rice noodles. Pantry is already fully stocked for this.
- Chicken piccata-ish — pound thin, sear, deglaze with lemon juice + splash of broth, swirl in butter. Over pasta or with bread. 20 min.
- Thai coconut soup — chicken broth (Better than Bullion) + coconut milk + curry paste + rice noodles + spinach + lime. 25 min. Pantry already stocked.

**Salmon:**
- Glazed salmon over rice — brush with chili garlic sauce + soy + honey + rice vinegar. 400°F, 12-14 min. Sheet pan. Done. Works when miso isn't on hand; chili garlic sauce is a solid alternative glaze.

**Other quick cooks:**
- Fried rice: leftover rice + fridge scraps + egg + soy sauce
- Quesadillas: cheese + whatever protein you have
- Eggs: frittata, shakshuka, loaded scramble
- Smash burgers — fast, minimal dishes

### Tier 3 — Dump and Bake / Hands-Off

- **Meatball pasta bake** (the classic) — frozen meatballs + jarred sauce + dry pasta + mozzarella. Gets 3 meals.
- **Baked gnocchi** — gnocchi + marinara + mozzarella, 400°F, 25-30 min. Even faster than pasta bake. Pantry already stocked.
- **White bean and sausage bake** — Italian sausage + 2 cans white beans + fire roasted tomatoes + broth + dried herbs + olive oil. Dutch oven or baking dish, 350°F, 45 min. Crusty bread. Pantry already stocked.
- **Chili** — ground beef or turkey (browning optional), chili beans + fire roasted tomatoes + spices. Dutch oven, 45 min simmer. Feeds 4+ meals.
- **Sheet pan sausage and potatoes** — Italian sausage sliced + potatoes cubed + olive oil + seasoning. 400°F, 35-40 min. Zero dishes.
- **Shakshuka** — fire roasted tomatoes + eggs cracked in + spices. 20 min, one pan. Breakfast-for-dinner energy.
- **Baby back ribs** — oven version: 275°F, 2.5-3 hours wrapped in foil, then 15 min uncovered with sauce. Weekend afternoon project, not a weeknight move. Weber kettle available once snow is off the deck (now accessible as of spring 2026).

- Chicken thighs braised in tomatoes and olives
- Lemon chicken with potatoes (dutch oven or sheet pan)
- Braised short ribs or chuck roast
- Baked salmon with sheet pan vegetables

## 6. Salad Bank

### Marty's Established Salads

- **Fattoush with feta and chicken** — her absolute favorite. Zing of the dressing is the point.
- **Chicken, bacon, blue cheese** — red onion, cucumber, tomato, balsamic vinaigrette, croutons if available
- **Chicken, dried cranberries, blue cheese or feta, walnuts or slivered almonds, vinaigrette** — can sub or add apples, pears, or strawberries. Very seasonal-flexible.

### New Summer Additions (Marty)

- **Grilled peach + chicken + arugula + goat cheese + candied pecans + balsamic** — peaches on the Foreman 3 min. Fattoush-adjacent energy, summery.
- **Watermelon + feta + cucumber + mint + red onion + lime vinaigrette** — lighter, no chicken needed. Add prosciutto for protein.
- **BLT salad** — romaine + bacon + tomato + avocado + croutons + blue cheese or caesar or ranch. Peak August tomato moment.
- **Mango + chicken + avocado + red onion + cilantro + lime** — deconstructed mango salsa over greens. Add jalapeño.
- **Fig + prosciutto + arugula + shaved parmesan + honey + balsamic** — no chicken, no cooking, feels fancy. Late summer.
- **Greek + chicken** — romaine + tomato + cucumber + red onion + kalamata + feta + oregano + red wine vinegar + olive oil. Already mostly stocked.

### Nick-Friendly Salads (More Conservative)

- **Traditional Greek** — as above, without the chicken if he prefers
- **Antipasto** — salami/pepperoni + provolone + pepperoncini + roasted red peppers + artichoke hearts + olives + Italian dressing. Over greens or not. Basically a charcuterie board over lettuce — highly Nick-compatible.

### Salad Strategy for Two
Build around a shared base, diverge on toppings. Chicken pre-cooked on the Foreman goes in both bowls. The split is just what lands on top. Antipasto is a good gateway for Nick — meat and cheese over lettuce.
